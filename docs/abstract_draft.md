# Abstract

**Abstract**—Predicting the outcomes of soccer matches for financial gain remains a formidable challenge due to the sport's high stochasticity and the efficiency of modern betting markets. Traditional machine learning approaches often fail to capture the complex, non-linear interactions between tactical concepts and lack the capacity for sequential financial decision-making. This paper introduces **HRM-DQN**, a novel reinforcement learning framework that integrates a Hierarchical Reasoning Model (HRM) with a Dueling Double Deep Q-Network. The architecture employs a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) to reason about abstract match dynamics (e.g., team form, offensive potency) before passing a hierarchical state representation to the betting agent. We evaluate this framework on a comprehensive dataset spanning three seasons (2022–2025) across the five major European leagues. Our experiments compare a generalized Global Model against specialized League-Specific agents. While the global approach failed to achieve profitability (-10.67% ROI), the league-specific agent for the **German Bundesliga** achieved a remarkable **+4.46% Return on Investment (ROI)** over the test season, with a maximum drawdown of less than 20%. These results empirically demonstrate that combining hierarchical concept reasoning with domain-specific training regimes can unlock profitable inefficiencies in sports betting markets, offering a robust alternative to traditional supervised learning methods.

**Keywords**—Deep Reinforcement Learning, Hierarchical Reasoning, Graph Convolutional Networks, Sports Analytics, Financial Forecasting, Soccer Prediction.
